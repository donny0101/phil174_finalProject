The Dutch government wanted to more efficiently analyze people applying for child care benefits through an machine learning algorithm that assessed applicants based on “risk indicators” that ended up punishing some families even if they had a false fraud allegation. This forced many families to give up their children to foster care as the algorithm wrongly classified these applicants as risky.

identifies at least two significant ethical and/or societal concerns about this collection & measurement system

One reason why the Dutch algorithm failed was miscommunication between the programming team and officials that used the system. The system accidentally emphasized some parts of the applicant's profile as riskier than necessary, causing unnecessary pain for the families involved.

provides responses to those concerns

The miscommunication problems seen in the Dutch childcare scandal aren’t unique. Whenever machine learning techniques are applied to real-world situations, the data that is put into the system may still create a bias as the system is working to solve one issue but may exaggerate current problems inherent in the system.
